The many discontinued cars of General Motors

What they were and why they were discontinued

Source - Index
General Motors, established in 1908, has had a plethora of car brands under its umbrella. The company was founded on the principle of acquiring a selection of brands, and placing them on a pricing ladder so customers could step up through the range as their needs and circumstances changed.

We're going to walk through them from the cool ones to the Extras

The cool ones

Pontiac


The Pontiac Firebird is an American automobile built and produced by Pontiac from the 1967 to 2002 model years.
Designed as a pony car to compete with the Ford Mustang, it was introduced on February 23, 1967, five months after GM's Chevrolet division's platform-sharing Camaro.

Hummer


Hummer is an American brand of pickups and SUVs first marketed in 1992 when AM General began selling a civilian version of the M998 Humvee.
Although discontinued in 2010, Hummer returned as a model under GMC in 2020.

Oldsmobile


Oldsmobile was a brand of American automobiles, produced for most of its existence by General Motors.
Founded in 1897 and defunct in 2004.

Acadian and Beaumont


Acadian is a make of automobile produced by General Motors of Canada from 1962 to 1971.
Beaumont was a make of mid-sized automobiles produced by General Motors of Canada from 1964 to 1969.

Saturn


The Saturn Corporation, also known as Saturn LLC, was an American automobile manufacturer, a registered trademark established in 1985 and defunct in 2010, as a subsidiary of General Motors.
Saturn attracted younger, budget-conscious buyers, building a loyal customer base and a memorable legacy in American automotive history.
