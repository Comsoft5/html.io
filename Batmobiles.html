The oldest Batmobiles

The BATMAN SERIAL (1943), The BATMAN AND ROBIN SERIAL (1949), The 1960s BATMAN SERIES

George Barris transformed Ford’s abandoned Futura concept car into the iconic Batmobile driven by Adam West in the 1960s Batman series. This Batmobile was packed with gadgets like an atomic turbine engine, rockets, lasers, and a rear-mounted parachute system, making it the ultimate crime-fighting vehicle. Although its partially exposed bubble tops made it less safe, its sleek black and red design with bat-wing fins made it a standout car of its time. The car later sold for $4.2 million, reflecting its iconic status. In contrast, earlier Batmobiles from the 1940s were just regular cars, like a ’39 Cadillac and a ’49 Mercury Convertible, with minimal features, serving as interesting relics of Batman's early days.
